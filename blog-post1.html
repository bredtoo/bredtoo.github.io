Why I chose ICT.

I made a decision on a whim, and I dont regret it.

When I graduated high school, I actually thought I was going to study something in the film industry or environmental field. I had always loved editing and content creation and wanted to take my already developing skills to the next level. So, when I took my gap year, I started making short-form content and streaming. Over time, I realized that, yes, I do love content creation and interacting with viewers, but my love for editing drifted away as the work got more complicated. So, I started looking for other study opportunities, but there was one problem: I wasn't really interested in anything. I looked at my grades from high school. 6.5 in biology — meh. 7 in science — no thanks. 8.5 in computer science — oh yeah! I was good at computer science, but I never really liked the subject. What pushed me to start applying was actually my friend taking this exact program at HZ University. He said he really enjoyed it, so I attended the open day and was thoroughly impressed! I loved the building, and the presentation was well done. I checked other colleges too, but none of them amounted to HZ for me personally. They usually specified in one single path like datascience, programming or AI specialist, but I didn't know what I wanted to do yet. So when i heard that HZ taught everything and gives the opportunity to specialize later on I knew this was the school for me. The biggest benefits for me were,

  • Travel isn't far.
  • Loved the building and first impressions.
  • I get to study something i'm decently good at.
  • One of my friends is going there anyways.
  • I get to explore the broader side of IT.

So I gave it a shot, and it's been really fun so far! We had to make a portfolio in just two weeks. It didn't have to be completely finished or look perfect at all, but I took it as a challenge and started working relentlessly on my project. I made a ton of mistakes, and not everything worked out the way I wanted it to, but the process was incredibly fun. I really discovered a passion for front-end development and I am constantly learning more!
