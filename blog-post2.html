Personal SWOT analysis.

My strengths and weaknesses

So a quick rundown for people who don't know what SWOT really is. SWOT stands for Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities and Threats. It's a tool that helps you take a step back and see what you're really all about. What are your strengths, what could use some work, what opportunities can you grab, and what challenges might be coming your way? You answer all these questions so that you can make smarter choices and build some self confidence.

Let's start with my strengths.

  • Solo work - I work well solo.
  • Pressure - I work decently well in high pressure situations like tests and exams.
  • Listening - I pay close attention to what people have to say.

I work the most comfortably alone. It's not that I don't like people, but when I have a clear vision, I just want things done my way. I work faster once I get the hang of things, and I am generally just more focused when I'm alone. I also don't like the expectations people set when you're in a group setting. I wouldn't say I work terribly in a group; I just work way more comfortably solo! I have also developed the wonderful ability to work well under pressure. Where other people freeze up or crack, I often thrive and perform better under a decent amount of pressure. No one likes being under pressure, but being able to work under it is a gift I have! Now, it might surprise you how many people are just terrible listeners. I always pay attention to what people teach me or have to say. I take a lot of notes during lectures so I know exactly what to expect and, more importantly, what to do. I take feedback very seriously and will always try to implement it in whatever way I can.

to my weaknesses!

  • Attendance - I have a bad history with attendance.
  • Slow learner - It takes a bit more time for me to understand things fully.
  • Time managment - I am terrible at planning.

My attendance has always been bad. I get so demotivated in the mornings to the point where I couldn't get out of bed. This is not just a laziness issue; I was dealing with some mental health challenges during that time as well. I hope that issue will not carry over to college, but the weakness is still there. Another weakness I have is that I learn relatively slowly. I like to take my time with the work that I do. I am usually the last person done with their work in a classroom. I just really like to make sure I fully understand what I'm doing. Now, my worst weakness is definitely time management. What are my opportunities?

  • A job - Multiple well paying jobs in the ICT sector.
  • Meet new people - International class means a lot of new people!
  • Building my portfolio - Getting knowledge in the ICT sector.

Of course, when looking at opportunities, one of the biggest is the availability of future work. With the growing demand in the ICT sector and the increasing number of people who avoid this field because they think AI will make jobs "not future-proof" (sorry for the rant), it's a great opportunity to pursue. HZ also offers internships and ensures you start building connections with companies so you can begin working as soon as you graduate! HZ offers the option to study in an international class! I love this choice because I enjoy meeting new people and getting to know them. That sadly doesn't mean I'm naturally good at it, though. But it's really fun hearing everyone's stories about where they're from, why they chose this subject, and why this location, etc. I've already met some really wonderful people and have been enjoying my class a lot! I didn't expect us to build a portfolio in the first couple of weeks of the program, but I was so glad we did! I had always wanted to make a portfolio but never really felt motivated enough. So, when we were tasked with creating a portfolio website, I was stoked! I really enjoyed building the website and hope I can keep developing it and adding more content!

And at last, what are my threats?

  • Burnout - 40+ hours a week of studying
  • Automation - Rapid development of AI
  • Market saturation - Sectors are getting highly crowded.

Burnout is a terrible feeling that no one likes to experience. Sadly, it is also a very common experience for people who work full time. Work continues to pile up, sleep keeps getting shorter and shorter, and before you know it, you just can't keep going. I'm scared that I will get overwhelmed with the amount of new knowledge I am expected to understand. But a good balance of work and free time should be prioritized in order to prevent it! Even though I called the people who believe the whole ICT sector will get automated by AI silly, some sectors really are being automated. These sectors usually involve repetitive tasks, but it's still something to keep in mind. Maybe I'll find something that I really enjoy doing, only to discover that it got automated. The main thing to take away from this is that people will always be needed in the ICT sector, but some jobs can be taken over by AI. Another thing that worries me is the amount of market saturation in the ICT sector. With so many people entering the field, competition for jobs and internships is getting tougher and tougher. On top of that, a lot of companies outsource work to cheaper labor markets, which makes it even harder to stand out. It's a little intimidating, and it means I really need to keep improving my skills and finding ways to separate myself from the crowd. And that's my whole SWOT analysis! Thank you for reading!
